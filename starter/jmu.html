James Madison University

James Madison University was founded in 1908 as the State Normal and Industrial School for Women at Harrisonburg. In honor of President James Madison, the institution was renamed Madison College in 1938, then named James Madison University in 1977.

JMU is an integral part of the Harrisonburg community. With student enrollment totalling aproxximately 21,000, JMU students make up 38% percent of the Harrisonburg population. More than half of JMU spending occurs within the Harrisonburg area, and JMU is a significant provider for the workforce needs of Harrisonburg.
